British Economic History Syllabus

3 Week 1: British economy and population: an overview

3.1 Core

  • Stephen Broadberry, Bruce M. S. Campbell, Alexander Klein, Mark Overton and Bas van Leeuwen, “Prologue: Historical national income accounting”, British Economic Growth, 1270-1870, CUP: 2015.
  • E. A. Wrigley, Chapter 2 “The divergence of England: the growth of the English economy in the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries,” in Poverty, Progress and Population, CUP: 2004.
  • Nicholas Crafts, “Forging Ahead and Falling Behind: The Rise and Relative Decline of the First Industrial Nation,” Journal of Economic Perspectives, Vol. 12, No. 2, 1998.

3.2 Additional

  • Wrigley, E. (2004). British population during the ‘long’ eighteenth century, 1680–1840. In R. Floud & P. Johnson (Eds.), The Cambridge Economic History of Modern Britain (pp. 57-95). Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.
  • R. Floud & D. McCloskey (eds), The Economic History of Britain since 1700 (2nd ed. 1994) vol. 1, ch.4 (Schofield)
  • P. Deane, The First Industrial Revolution (1965) ch. 2
  • M.W. Flinn, English Population Growth 1700-1830 (1970)
  • Morgan Kelly, Cormac Ó Gráda, Adam Smith, Watch Prices, and the Industrial Revolution , The Quarterly Journal of Economics, Volume 131, Issue 4, November 2016, Pages 1727–1752
  • Gregory Clark, “The condition of the working class in England, 1209-2004,” Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 113, No. 6, 2005
  • D. McCloskey, “1780-1860: a survey”, in R. Floud & D. McCloskey (eds.) The Economic History of Britain Since 1700, 2nd Ed. CUP: 1994.
