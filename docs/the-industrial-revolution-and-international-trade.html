8 The industrial revolution and international trade

8.1 Core

  • Ronald Findlay and Kevin O’Rourke, Chapter 6 “Trade and the Industrial Revolution” in Power and Plenty: Trade, War, and the World Economy in the Second Millenium, PUP: 2007.
  • Joseph Inikori, Chapter 3 “A Historiography of the first industrial revolution” in Africans and the Industrial Revolution, CUP: 2002.
  • Nuala Zahedieh, “Eric Williams and William Forbes: copper, colonial markets, and commercial capitalism”, The Economic History Review, Vol. 74, No. 3, p. 784-808

8.2 Additional

  • Harley, C. (2004). Trade: Discovery, mercantilism and technology. In R. Floud & P. Johnson (Eds.), The Cambridge Economic History of Modern Britain (pp. 175-203). Cambridge: Cambridge University Press. {target=“_blank”}
  • Engerman, S., & O’Brien, P. (2004). The industrial revolution in global perspective. In R. Floud & P. Johnson (Eds.), The Cambridge Economic History of Modern Britain (pp. 451-464). Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.
  • J. Price, ‘What did Merchants Do? Reflections on British Overseas Trade 1660-1790’ Journal of Economic History (1989)
  • D. Barnett, London: Hub of the Industrial Revolution (1998) Introduction and ch. 6
  • S. Chapman, Merchant Enterprise in Britain from the Industrial Revolution to World War 1 (1992) ch.3
