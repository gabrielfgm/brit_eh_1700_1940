11  19th century globalization and trade

The enormous growth in industrial capacity in the 19th century had global consequences. Within Britain, the political equilibrium moved towards the full embrace of free trade. Globally, industrial technologies such as steam were pushing down transportation costs driving the integration of global markets. The result was a sharp growth in global trade and the convergence of global commodity prices which resulted in both improved purchasing power for British labor, and declining profitability for the British agricultural sector.
