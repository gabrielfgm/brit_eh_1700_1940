British Economic History Syllabus

6 Causes of the industrial revolution

6.1 Core

  • Robert Allen, “Why the industrial revolution was British: commerce, induced invention, and the scientific revolution,” Economic History Review, Vol. 64, No. 2, 2011.
  • Joel Mokyr, The Enlightened Economy: An Economic History of Britain 1700-1850, Yale: 2009, Introduction and Chapter 5.
  • Patrick O’Brien, “A conjuncture in global history or an Anglo-American construct: the British Industrial Revolution, 1700–1850,” Journal of Global History, Vol. 5, No. 3, 2010.
  • Jane Humphries, “The lure of aggregates and the pitfalls of the patriarchal perspective: a critique of the high wage economy interpretation of the British industrial revolution,” Economic History Review, Vol. 66, no. 3, 2013.

6.2 Additional

  • Nicholas Crafts, “Explaining the first Industrial Revolution: two views,” European Review of Economic History, Vol. 15, pp. 153-168, 2010.
  • Stephenson, J. Z. (2018), ‘Real’ wages? Contractors, workers, and pay in London building trades, 1650–1800. The Economic History Review, 71: 106-132. {target="_blank"}
  • Allen, R. C. (2019), Real wages once more: a response to Judy Stephenson. The Economic History Review, 72: 738-754.
  • Humphries, J. and Schneider, B. (2019), Spinning the industrial revolution. The Economic History Review, 72: 126-155.
  • Allen, R. C. (2019), Spinning their wheels: a reply to Jane Humphries and Benjamin Schneider. The Economic History Review.
  • Morgan Kelly, Joel Mokyr, and Cormac O Grada, “Precocious Albion: A New Interpretation of the British Industrial Revolution,” Annual Review of Economics, Vol. 6, 2014.
